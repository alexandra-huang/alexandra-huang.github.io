CSE 3/7/5A Course Websites

I developed and redesigned the CSE 3 (Fluency in Information Technology), CSE 5A (Intro to Programming), and CSE 7 (Intro to Programming in MATLAB) websites for Professor Susan Marx. The previous websites were outdated and not responsive, and navigation was difficult because there was no way to get back to the previous page for some of the pages without using the back button; the design was inconsistent. The new design gives the websites color (since the previous one was very gray), and the color scheme has a professional look that ties the course websites back to UCSD. The websites were made responsive using Bootstrap.

Note: The course websites are hosted by UCSD, and as such, are only live during the quarters the courses are offered.

ItzLit

ItzLit was implemented with Express, NodeJs, MongoDB, the SpotCrime API, the Google Maps API, and OAuth to incorporate Facebook Login, using street light data available online from the county of San Diego. ItzLit is a web application created with the intent of allowing people to find the most well-lit route home (within San Diego County), thus encouraging a sense of safety in the users of the application.

The problem my team identified and wanted to address was that of students and staff who have to commute to and from campus, often times leaving campus late at night, and because people feel less safe traveling at night alone, the idea for ItzLit was born. ItzLit also helps people with poor night vision to find a more well-lit route to drive on, as there are options that the user can pick for mode of transportation when inputting their starting and ending locations. Data visualizations made using D3 were incorporated using crime data from the UCSD Delphi Database to provide users with visualizations for the types of crimes that have occurred in San Diego and thus to assess past crime history in the area. The SpotCrime API was used to get live crime data that is displayed on the map showing the different routes so that users can make their decision of what route to take based on how well-lit the route is as well as what types of crimes have recently occurred.

Team Members: Leon Li, Carolyn Thio, Justin Lee, John Gamboa, Crystal Nguyen, and Alexandra Huang (self)
